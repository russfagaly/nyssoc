This Day

June
11

1776 — Congress appoints the Committee of Five.

The Continental Congress names John Adams, Benjamin Franklin, Thomas Jefferson, Robert R. Livingston of New York, and Roger Sherman to draft a declaration of independence. Jefferson takes up the pen; within a month, the Declaration is adopted.
