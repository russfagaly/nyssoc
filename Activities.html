Educational & Charitable Activities

The founding principles of friendship, memory, and charity drive everything the Society does today.

At Mount Gulian

The Cincinnati Gallery

A permanent gallery dedicated to the New York State Society at the Mount Gulian historic site — displays, artifacts, and documents illustrating the Society's founding and its continuous existence since 1783.

Education

Teacher of the Year

An annual award honoring New York teachers who bring the history of the American Revolution alive in the classroom. Learn about the program.

Fellowship

Annual Meeting

Members meet each year at the Verplanck homestead — Baron von Steuben's Revolutionary headquarters overlooking the Hudson at Beacon, New York, which looks much as it did in 1783.

Commemoration

The 250th Anniversary

As the nation marks the semiquincentennial of the Revolution, the Society takes part in commemorations of New York's defining events — from the Battle of Brooklyn to Evacuation Day.

Research

Genealogy & History

The Society assists researchers tracing the officers of the New York Line, and maintains resources for genealogical and historical inquiry. Browse resources.

Charity

Members in Need

True to the third founding principle, the Society stands ready to assist members, or families of members, who may be in need.
