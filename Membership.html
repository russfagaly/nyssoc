Membership in the Society

Membership is based on descent from a New York officer of the Continental Army, Navy, or Marine Corps who served at least three years, was an Original Member of the Society, or died in service.

The Path to Election

A series of steps toward admission.

The final decision for admission rests with the membership of the Society, but a series of intermediate actions should be followed prior to election at one of its formal meetings. Only one person may represent an officer as a full hereditary member at any one time; where the most direct and senior line cannot be identified, a collateral line is acceptable.

  1. Determine your eligibility

    Review your genealogy and the service record of your ancestors. Membership rests on descent from a qualifying New York officer of the Revolution.

  2. Contact the Secretary

    If you believe yourself eligible, write to the Secretary of the Society for advice and assistance at Info@nycincinnati.org.

  3. Claims & Admissions review

    If your line looks promising, the Secretary will direct your inquiry to the Claims and Admissions Committee for further evaluation.

  4. Attend a meeting

    Prior to any formal application, you will be invited to attend one of the regular meetings of the Society — the better to get to know the members, and the members, you.

  5. Authenticate your lineage

    Applicants should be prepared to document their descent with the standard genealogical proofs before election at a formal meeting.

Beyond eligibility, a key element of membership is a genuine commitment to the original goals of the Cincinnati: honoring those who sacrificed so much to bring us independence — particularly George Washington — and keeping alive a clear picture of the history of the American Revolution.
