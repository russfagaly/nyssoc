Teacher of the Year

Honoring the New York teachers who keep a clear picture of the American Revolution alive for the next generation.

Of the Society's three founding principles, none speaks more directly to the present than the charge to “keep alive the memory of what they did.” The Teacher of the Year award recognizes educators across New York State whose teaching of the American Revolution does exactly that — with rigor, imagination, and devotion to their students.

The award

Each year the Society honors a New York teacher whose work brings the Revolutionary era alive in the classroom. The recipient is recognized by the Society, and the award is presented at one of its formal gatherings.

Nominations

Teachers may be nominated by colleagues, administrators, students, or parents — and educators are welcome to put themselves forward. Nominations should describe how the candidate teaches the Revolution: the courses, projects, and approaches that set their work apart.

To nominate a teacher, write to the Society at Info@nycincinnati.org.
