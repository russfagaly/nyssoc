Est. June 9, 1783 · Society of Friends

Keeping alive the spirit and principles of the American Revolution.

The New York State Society of the Cincinnati was formed by the officers of the Continental Line to honor the memory of George Washington, to perpetuate their friendships, and to assist members in need — principles that drive our educational and charitable work to this day.

Our Founding Principles

Three promises, kept since 1783.

I — Friendship

Perpetuate the fellowship

To preserve among members the friendships first established by Washington's original Line officers, continued today by their descendants.

II — Memory

Keep alive the memory

To honor the sacrifices of the founding generation and keep a clear picture of the history of the American Revolution before the public.

III — Charity

Assist those in need

To provide support to members, or the families of members, when they may be in need — and to extend that charitable spirit outward.

About the Society

Interwoven with the history of the nation.

Our members were founders of the United States — in the Revolutionary War and in the framing of the Constitution. Of about 453 New York officers who qualified in 1783, 235 became Original Members; their descendants have included presidents, justices, generals, and ambassadors.

Each year the Society meets at the Verplanck homestead — Baron von Steuben's Revolutionary headquarters overlooking the Hudson at Beacon, New York, now the Mount Gulian historic site.

Membership

Descended from a New York officer of the Revolution?

Membership is based on descent from a New York officer of the Continental Army, Navy, or Marine Corps who served at least three years, was an Original Member, or died in service. One member represents each officer at a time.
